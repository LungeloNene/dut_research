Overview

As part of the Research and Postgraduate Support Team, the main objective of the Research Capacity Development (RCD) Office is to develop research capacity at the university, with a specific focus on emerging researchers. The RCD office uses a multipronged approach to do this. The following approaches are used

  1. Growing the number of staff and postgraduate students who publish.
  2. Facilitating supervisors training.
  3. Growing the number of staff who have doctorates.
  4. Providing support for emerging researchers.
  5. Providing research skills training and support for postgraduate students.

 

Research Capacity Development Workshops

In an attempt to boost the research capacity of DUT staff and students, the following workshops are organised periodically:

  • Preparing or writing up a research proposal
  • Developing a conceptual framework for postgraduate studies
  • Data gathering, analysis and processes
  • Research reading skills
  • Writing a literature review
  • EndNote
  • SPSS Workshop
  • Using citation and referencing conventions
  • Research paradigms
  • Writing a thesis
  • Academic writing: writing for publications
  • PowerPoint for Effective Presentations

 

Doctoral Mentoring Workshop

The doctoral mentoring workshop presents an opportunity for doctoral students to work on their research proposals and dissertation in small groups with experienced and acclaimed scholars. Doctoral candidates also have the opportunity to discuss and engage with peers on research, theoretical and methodological interests. Doctoral candidates receive detailed feedback on the several stages of their research proposal and dissertation.

 

Interdisciplinary Research and Innovation Conference

The Interdisciplinary Research and Innovation Conference is an annual event hosted by the Research and Postgraduate Support Directorate. Since the inception of the conference in 2016, we have welcomed both national and international researchers, innovators, postgraduate students and academics to exchange ideas from diverse disciplines such as: Accounting, Management Sciences, Humanities, Arts, Design, Sciences, Health Sciences, Engineering, and Information Technology. For more information about the conference, please send a mail to abstracts@dut.ac.za

 

Strengthening Postgraduate Supervision

This innovative short course is offered through Rhodes University. The three-phase course provided structured support needed by academics as they take on the complex and demanding role of doctoral and masters supervision. The phases consisted of two face-to-face workshops and one online component. The course combines discussions, case studies, research literature, video clips, and numerous other activities and information, both online and face-to-face, to help the new supervisor engage with the practice and processes of doctoral supervision in a reflective and informative way.

 

Research and Doctoral Leadership Academy (RADLA)

The RADLA is an initiative set up to support academics who have already obtained their PhDs or have registered for a PhD. The academy works closely with academics in a bid to assist in managing and supporting their careers. The workshops, seminars, public lectures, and training linked to RADLA include:

  • Understanding the higher education landscape: local and global.
  • Managing your academic career from post doc to professor including developing a publication plan
  • Ethics of publishing
  • NRF rating workshops
  • Writing for publications and getting published
  • Supervisor support workshops
  • Contributing as a public intellectual
  • Success with research grants: the entrepreneurial research environment in a changing landscape.

The RADLA initiative is led by Professor Cheryl Potgieter.
